Senior Design Project

Fall 2024

This semester, I began working on my senior design with a team of 3 other electrical engineering students. We are working on the project for our client, Honeywell National Security Campus. The premise of the project is to develop destructive and non-destructing evaluation methods in order to test a small deviceâ€™s electrical and mechanical properties. While the specific device in question is not of particular interest, the one we are working on for this project is a small rubber toy with a circuit inside it that flashes a pattern of LEDs on and off whenever a sufficiently strong external force is applied.

In addition to creating evaluation methods to test for any defects, we are also trying to create methods to analyze whether the device been tampered with, as this is a crucial in areas of national security. This is where my personal role in the project comes in. I am currently working on swapping the integrated circuit within the device with a different one that will manipulate the functionality of the electric circuit. This will emulate the act of an adversary tampering with the device. Following the successful swap of the ICs, I will be able to test methods for detecting whether this tampering has occurred.

This project ha incorporated and developed several skills in engineering disciplines. First of all, the IC I am working with is a very small, very basic microcontroller. This means I am able to incorporate my embedded systems knowledge. Additionally, this project has also required me to take many electrical measurements and has such strengthened my skills in using electrical testing equipment.

The big picture of this project is to promote the creation of general evaluation methods for small electronic and mechanical systems. This is a topic of critical importance in national security which is why Honeywell is pursuing it with us.
